International Organization for Standardization (ISO)

The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) is a worldwide group that makes rules to ensure products and services are safe and good quality. These rules cover many areas, like how things are made, how businesses manage the environment, and how they keep information secure. Following ISO rules can help companies show they're trustworthy, reach new customers, and make their operations better.

ISO 9001

ISO 9001 is a global standard that helps organizations ensure they deliver quality products and services consistently. It's about meeting customer needs, improving processes, involving employees, and always striving to do better. Getting certified means proving you're committed to quality.

Our Services



  1. Penetrant Testing (PT)

    Penetrant Testing (PT) is a way to find flaws on material surfaces without damaging them. It involves applying a colored liquid that seeps into any cracks or holes, drawn in by capillary action, which is like a liquid's ability to sneak into tight spaces. After some time, excess liquid is wiped away, and a powder is added to draw out the colored liquid from any flaws. This makes the flaws visible, helping to ensure the material's quality and safety.

  2. Magnetic Particle Testing (MPT)

    Magnetic Particle Testing (MPT)/(MT), also known as Magnetic Particle inspection (MPI), is a non-destructive testing (NDT) method used for detecting surface and near-surface flaws in ferromagnetic materials. It relies on the principle that magnetic fields are distorted by the presence of a discontinuity or defect in the material. MT is commonly used in various industries for quality control and inspection of components.

  3. Ultrasonic Testing (UT)

    Ultrasonic Testing (UT) is a method to check materials for flaws without causing damage. It uses high-frequency sound waves sent into the material. When these waves hit any changes like cracks or boundaries, they bounce back. A special device then analyzes these echoes to find and assess any flaws. UT is commonly used in industries like manufacturing and construction to check for defects in materials like metal and plastic. It's popular because it's precise and can find flaws deep inside materials.




    Related video:


  4. Radiographic Testing (RT)

    Radiographic Testing (RT) is a non-destructive testing method used to examine the internal structure of materials for flaws or defects. It works by passing X-rays or gamma rays through the material being tested onto a film or detector placed on the opposite side. Areas of the material that are thicker or denser, such as welds or internal defects, will absorb more of the radiation and appear as darker areas on the film or detector. By analyzing these images, technicians can detect defects like cracks, voids, or inclusions within the material. RT is commonly used in industries such as aerospace, construction, and manufacturing to ensure the integrity and safety of critical components, welds, and structures.

  5. Industrial Rope Access Trade Association (IRATA)

    The Industrial Rope Access Trade Association (IRATA) is an international organization that sets standards and provides training for industrial rope access technicians. These technicians use ropes and specialized equipment to work at heights and in difficult-to-reach areas, such as offshore structures, bridges, and industrial facilities. IRATA promotes safety, professionalism, and best practices within the rope access industry by establishing guidelines for training, equipment inspection, and operational procedures. Members of IRATA, including training companies and rope access technicians, adhere to these standards to ensure safe and efficient work at height.
